# Office Layout Requirements — 3rd Floor

## Company

- **Type**: SaaS startup
- **Current team**: 28 people
- **Target capacity**: 35–40 seated + standing desks
- **Floor**: 3rd floor of a commercial building
- **Lunch**: Separate 4th floor (not part of this layout)

---

## Building Structure

### Floor Shape
- **L-shaped** (NOT a simple rectangle)
- **Main office rectangle**: ~13m wide (E–W) × ~20m deep (N–S)
- **Bottom-left extension**: Pantry + WC (~5m × 2.5m)
- **Bottom-right extension**: Entry area with glass facade (~8m × 5m)
- **Total area**: ~3,300 sq ft (~306 sq m)

### Ceiling
- ~3.2m height
- Exposed structural beams, painted dark/black
- Industrial look — only natural light from side windows and south glass facade
- **Requires pendant/hanging lights throughout** (no overhead natural light)

### Structural Columns (Pillars)
- **West wall**: 4 thick rectangular columns (~60cm × 40cm), ~1ft gap from wall
- **East wall**: 6 thick rectangular columns (~60cm × 40cm), ~1ft gap from wall
- Columns painted dark/black
- Ceiling beams run E–W connecting opposite column pairs
- **1ft gap between pillar and wall** = usable for built-in cupboards/storage

### Walls & Windows
- **North wall**: 13m wide, fully solid, no windows, no doors, blue-grey paint
- **West wall**: 3 windows (grilled, between the 4 pillars)
- **East wall**: 5 windows (grilled, between the 6 pillars)
- **South wall**: Full glass facade with panoramic views, single wooden entry door

### Entry & Access
- **Lift + Stairs**: Building core, OUTSIDE the office (to the left when approaching)
- **Entry door**: Single door in the glass facade
- **Entry path**: Exit lift/stairs → move left → reach the glass door
- **When entering**: You face the east wall (6-pillar side)
- **Glass facade**: Extends across the south wall of the entry extension, with door roughly center-left

### Existing Permanent Structures
- **Washrooms**: 2 stalls, inside the office (SW area, behind lift shaft) — separate enclosed room
- **Pantry**: Adjacent to WC, separate enclosed room — west side of the extension
- Both are existing and permanent (may be relocated later)

## Design & Aesthetic Requirements

### Lighting
- **Pendant/hanging lights** required throughout (dark ceiling, limited overhead natural light)
- Natural light sources: 3 west windows + 5 east windows + south glass facade
- Suggested: Track lighting or pendant clusters at each pillar bay
- ~15 pendant lights across the workspace

### Materials & Finishes
- **Ceiling**: Keep exposed dark beams (industrial aesthetic)
- **Floor**: Marble/tile (existing, visible in site photos)
- **Pillars**: Dark painted (existing)
- **CXO cabin fronts**: Glass partitions (transparency, visibility)
- **Meeting rooms**: Glass or semi-glass partitions
- **Conference room**: Takes advantage of existing glass facade

### Acoustic Zoning
- **South = Active zone**: Entry, cafeteria, conference, WC
- **Center = Collaborative**: Open workspace, desk pods
- **North = Quiet zone**: CXO cabins, collab zone, call pods
- Call rooms and meeting rooms **distributed** (not clustered) for even coverage

## Key Design Decisions & Rationale

1. **CXO cabins in pillar bays (not all at the back)**: Pillars = free walls, each cabin gets a window, leaders sit among the team (visible + accessible)
2. **Conference in glass entry extension**: Clients go straight there, never cross workspace. Premium glass views.
3. **Call rooms distributed on 3 walls**: Even coverage, no queuing at one location
4. **East wall alternating pattern** (Huddle→CFO→Sync→CTO): Each CXO has a meeting room next door
5. **Standing desks in pillar bays**: Adds 6 work positions using window recesses, zero floor space impact
6. **North wall for windowless rooms**: Booth, Focus, Server/Prayer don't need windows; freed wall = whiteboard surface
7. **Built-in cupboards in pillar gaps**: Uses the dead 1ft space between pillars and walls for storage
8. **No reception/lobby**: SaaS company, not needed — corridor flows directly to workspace
